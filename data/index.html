What can we gleam from data?

I am constantly fascinated about the amount of data that our technology-driven society produces. The creation of so much data offers a myriad of both problems and opportunities.

One of the hardest parts of working with data is its extraction and sanitization. It is perfectly reasonable to be working on a data set that consists of billions of entries, and without smart techniques to obtain, store, and work with this data any analysis will be inefficient or impossible.

Once a data set has been obtained, then analysis can begin to see what can be inferred from the data. Initially this may involve computing various metrics and statistics related to the data set. With more sophisticated techniques, however, it is possible to extend this analysis to predict behaviour of hypothetical data (in the future or outside of the data's environment). These predictions can be validated and tested against, given enough time or data, and can be useful for many applications.
